Andrew Lin

hi! i am andrew lin, a third-year undergraduate student at the university of chicago. i major in sociology and media arts and design, and i am very passionate about games and play. i seek to both examine them as cultural objects, and try to incorporate my own reflections of sociology into my design process.

i have worked on quite a few team projects, mostly during my coursework. i really value coordination and communication in the game design process. i am also eager to apply my academic learnings, especially my study of the sociology of culture, in real-world contexts.

i am also learning to make websites and thus potentially incorporate them into transmedia projects.

please feel free to browse through my portfolio to see some projects i worked on/am working on! they're mostly group projects - please click on their links for more information.

The Red Divide - Great Purge

the red divide is a 3-player deck-building game. two opposition players, each on their own, vie to coup stalin and gain control by securing a majority in supreme soviet. there's no alliance between them; the first to overthrow stalin wins. the third player, as stalin, aims to thwart both. drawing inspiration from dou dizhu, domination, and avalon, the game features a distinct 2v1 dynamic.

(Don't) Fear the Reaper

(don't) fear the reaper is a tabletop RPG inspired by the quiet year and the blue öyster cult classic. players play as interstellar civilizations within one single galaxy. shaping a story that spans three thousand years of growth and conflict, players must all the while face the threat of outside forces and a looming sense of doom.

Slow and Steady Wins the (Drag) Race

slow and steady is a cooperative deck-building game for 3-4 players. players work together to help mary shelley, their turtle friend, come out of their shell, express their inner drag queen, and compete in a ✨drag race! players help mary overcome social pressures and boost their confidence.

Suspect Pursuit

suspect pursuit is my first attempt in creating a puzzle with physical objects. centered around train tickets, the puzzle also tries to explore the creative use of qr codes.

Café énigme

welcome to café énigme, a puzzle that involves coffee cups, *actual* macarons, study notes, and all the things you need when studying at a café. can you find the hidden message behind everything?

Left Behind

left behind is a room escape game about death and mourning, about how someone's physical possessions serve as powerful mementos of their lifetime after their passing.
