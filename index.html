about me

hi! i am andrew lin, a third-year undergraduate student at the university of chicago. i major in sociology and media arts and design, and i am very passionate about games and play. i seek to both examine them as cultural objects, and try to incorporate my own reflections of sociology into my design process.

i have mostly worked on team projects and i really value coordination and communication in the game design process. i am also eager to apply my academic learnings, especially my study of the sociology of culture, in real-world contexts.

i am also learning to make websites and thus potentially incorporate them into transmedia projects.

please feel free to browse through my portfolio to see some projects i worked on/am working on! they're mostly group projects - please click on their links for more information.

The Red Divide

the red divide is a 3-player card game in which two players compete against each other, while also working to overthrow the third player who tries to stay in power. inspired in part by traditional chinese card game dou dizhu and other card games such as domination.

(Don't) Fear the Reaper

(don't) fear the reaper is a tabletop game inspired by the quiet year and, of course, the blue öyster cult classic. players play as interstellar civilizations within one single galaxy. shaping a story that spans three thousand years of growth and conflict, players must all the while face the threat of outside forces and a looming sense of doom.

Slow and Steady Wins the (Drag) Race

slow and steady is a cooperative deck-building game for 3-4 players. players work together to help mary shelley, their turtle friend, come out of their shell, express their inner drag queen, and compete in a ✨drag race! players help mary overcome social pressures and boost their confidence.
